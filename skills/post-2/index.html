I started off by watching a Lynda Video about how Advertising Campaigns need to tell a story, but not always the whole story. If you leave it open-ended, the customer will want to finish it on their own. for instance, if you generate the need to purchase a product, and justify that need, but omit the character actually buying the product, the customer will feel the need to finish the story and purchase the product on their own.

After watching this video, I felt the need to get some more hands-on practice with Photoshop and some of its features. My first project I came up with was to incorporate some of the elements of the Pittsburgh skyline into an image of London, because I am from Pittsburgh, and studied abroad in London over the summer. This place would be my ideal city. I put the Cathedral of Learning, the Highmark Building, PPG Building, U.S. Steel Building, One Oxford Center, and BNY Mellon Building behind an image of Big Ben. This gave me a lot of practice with cutting an element out of its overall image, using the Magic Wand and Quick Selection tools. Once I pasted them into the Big Ben picture, I had to alter some of their color attributes to create a slight sunset effect. Overall, I am pleased with the outcome, given my level of experience.

My next thought was to make a graphic for the Pitt-PSU rivalry football game coming up this fall. I wanted to create the effect of both head coaches coming out of a cloud of smoke the color of their respective teams. Again I had to cut the coaches out of a separate image, and this time I had to create Layers of smoke so that some of it was in front of the coach, and some of it was behind them. I did not get very far with this project before joining Creative 360 and acquiring some assigned work.

On February 22nd, I joined a club at Pitt called Creative 360. This club is a student-run consulting group that creates logos, fliers, websites, etc. for other groups on campus who do not have the in-house expertise, or cannot afford to pay someone for this work. My first project was to create a logo for the Pitt Rocketry Club. The president of this club had many requirements for his new logo, including bright colors, their slogan, and a subtle Pitt reference. At this point, my job was to create a couple of different templates to get a feel for what he liked and disliked and then we would create a finalized logo soon after. This is still a work in progress.
